The Necrotropolis was typically a quiet, orderly place. The streets, paved with cool, damp stone, were free of the frantic rushing characteristic of the Before Times. Citizens moved with a deliberate, dragging shuffle, finding contentment in their slow, unhurried routines. Society functioned smoothly. The Rot was celebrated, the Hunger was managed with synthetic marrow rations, and the concept of 'death' was a quaint historical curiosity. 

Mayor Grimsby, a distinguished gentleman whose jawbone was held on by a tasteful silk cravat, was reviewing the municipal slime-drainage reports when the alarm sounded. It was a piercing, frantic wail that hadn't been heard in a century. 

He shuffled to the window overlooking the public square. Down below, panic was spreading. Citizens were stopping their aimless wandering, groaning in alarm. 

"Sir! Sir!" Officer Marrow burst into the office, holding his left arm awkwardly, as it had become detached at the elbow during his rush. 

"What is the meaning of this commotion, Marrow?" Grimsby asked slowly.

"It's... it's the Humans, sir. An outbreak."

Grimsby’s one good eye widened. "Impossible. We eradicated the affliction generations ago. They were too violent, too chaotic."

"They're back, sir. In Sector 4. It started with a family of four who woke up... warm, sir. Warm and breathing." Marrow shuddered, an impressive feat given his lack of muscle tension. "They started displaying extreme symptoms. Running. Talking loudly. Demanding 'coffee' and 'mortgages'. Their skin... it’s pink, sir. Pink and smooth."

"By the Ancestors," Grimsby murmured. "Are they contagious?"

"Highly. A citizen got too close, trying to offer them some soothing carrion, and they bit him. Not to eat, sir! Out of pure, irrational aggression. They called him a 'monster'." Marrow reattached his arm with a grimace. "The citizen retreated, but within an hour, his rot reversed. His heart started beating. He’s now pacing angrily around his crypt, asking where his tax dollars are going."

Grimsby grabbed his mayoral staff. "We must contain this. The infrastructure of Necrotropolis cannot support the living. They consume too many resources, create too much noise, and their obsession with 'productivity' will destroy our peaceful stagnation."

He hurried, navigating the stairwell with careful, deliberate steps. The streets were in chaos. A mob of newly reverted humans was marching down the main boulevard, chanting slogans about human rights and demanding the opening of organic supermarkets. They were terrifyingly fast, their eyes wide with frantic, pulsing life. 

The undead citizens cowered in alleyways, clutching their detached limbs in terror. 

"Order!" Grimsby bellowed, his vocal cords struggling to produce the volume. "We must not let the Life spread! Maintain social distancing! Do not let them breathe on you!"

But it was too late. A human, clad in business casual, sprinted toward Grimsby, holding a clipboard and looking dangerously enthusiastic. "Excuse me! Do you have a minute to talk about our civic revitalization initiative?"

Grimsby let out a long, low moan of despair. The End Times had returned, and it looked like a homeowner’s association.
