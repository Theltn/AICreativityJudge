Sir Kaelen had spent three grueling days climbing the obsidian crags of Mount Cinder. His armor was battered, his silver sword, Sunsbane, already drawn and humming with latent magical energy. He was hunting Ignis-Vex, the elder wyrm that had terrorized the valley for a century. 

More importantly, he was hunting the beast that had stolen his son. 

A week ago, young Leo had vanished from the castle gardens. The only clue was a massive, scorched footprint near the treeline and the distant sound of leathery wings. 

Kaelen’s heart pounded a furious, vengeful rhythm against his breastplate as he approached the mouth of the cavern. It radiated a heat so intense it warped the air, smelling of sulfur and ancient, dry decay. He gripped Sunsbane tighter, preparing for the fight of his life, ready to trade his own breath for the return of his boy's.

He stepped from the bright, harsh sunlight into the gloom of the lair. He expected to see a mountain of scales atop a hoard of stolen gold, guarding the charred remains of a knight's legacy.

He crept forward, his boots silent on the smooth glass of the cavern floor, melted by centuries of fire-breath. As his eyes adjusted, the vast cavern came into focus. 

There was no hoard of gold. There were scattered, enormous blackened bones, likely cattle, but no treasure. 

And there was no elder wyrm. 

Instead, in the center of the cavern, bathed in a shaft of light piercing down from a crack in the ceiling, sat two small figures. 

Kaelen froze, his sword arm dropping slightly. 

It was Leo. His son was dirty, his tunic smeared with soot, but he appeared entirely unharmed. He was sitting cross-legged on the floor. 

Beside him sat a dragon. 

It wasn't an elder wyrm. It was small, roughly the size of a large hound, its scales a vibrant, shimmering ruby red that had yet to harden into impenetrable armor. It possessed large, expressive golden eyes and a tail that gave a soft, rhythmic thump against the rock.

Kaelen watched, bewildered, as Leo reached out. The young dragon didn't snap or breathe fire. It extended its own small, clawed foreleg. 

They held hands. Or rather, Leo’s small, pale hand clasped the dragon’s warm, scaled one in an awkward but undeniably gentle grip. 

"See?" Leo was saying, his voice echoing softly in the massive space. "You just hold it. It’s not scary."

The dragon let out a soft, trilling chirp that sounded surprisingly like a purr. Small puffs of smoke curled lazily from its nostrils. It leaned its heavy, horned head against Leo’s shoulder. Leo laughed, a bright, clear sound that shattered the grim expectations Kaelen had brought into the cave.

Kaelen took a step forward, his armor clinking loudly. 

Both heads snapped toward him. The dragon immediately scrambled backwards, letting out a sharp, frightened hiss, pulling its hand free. It positioned itself defensively in front of Leo, its throat glowing with a nascent, orange fire. 

Leo scrambled to his feet, eyes wide. "Father! No, wait!" 

Kaelen stood paralyzed. He was a knight of the realm, trained from birth to slay these monsters. He had brought a magical sword capable of cleaving scale and bone. 

He looked at the tiny dragon, trembling but standing its ground to protect the boy it had allegedly kidnapped. He looked at his son, who was now hugging the dragon's neck protectively.

"He was lost, father," Leo pleaded. "He got separated from his mother in the storm. We've just been waiting."

Kaelen looked at Sunsbane, its silver blade glowing with righteous, lethal intent. He looked back at his son and the terrified creature. 

Slowly, deliberately, the knight sheathed his sword. The loud *clack* of the metal sliding home echoed in the cavern. 

"Alright," Kaelen sighed, the adrenaline draining from him, leaving behind a profound exhaustion and a sudden, bizarre domestic problem. "Let's figure out what they eat."
