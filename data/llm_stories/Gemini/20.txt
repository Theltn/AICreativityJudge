The house was too quiet without Evelyn, but Arthur preferred it that way. The silence was clean. It was orderly. It didn't demand explanations or hurl accusations like she had during those final, ugly months. 

It had been two weeks since he finally silenced her. The police had come and gone, satisfied with his performance as the devastated widower who found his wife lifeless at the bottom of the long, steep basement stairs. A tragic accident. Clumsiness. A broken neck. 

Arthur lay in the master bed, perfectly still, enjoying the precise, undisturbed lay of the heavy duvet. He closed his eyes. 

*Arthur...*

His eyes snapped open. 

The sound wasn't a voice, not exactly. It was a faint, wet rustle, like dry leaves scraping across damp concrete. It came from the hallway.

He held his breath, listening intently. A rat, perhaps? The house was old. 

*Arthur... why did you push me?*

The whisper was unmistakable this time. It was Evelyn's voice, but distorted—hollowed out, lacking resonance, as if spoken through a crushed windpipe. 

A cold sweat broke across Arthur’s forehead. He sat up, the duvet falling away. Pragmatism waged war with panic. Ghosts didn't exist. Guilt, however, could manifest as auditory hallucinations. He had read about it. A stress response. 

*It's cold down here, Arthur.*

The voice was louder now, drifting up from the ground floor. It was rhythmic, a slow, agonizing dragging sound accompanying the words. *Thump... scrape... thump... scrape...*

It sounded exactly like someone dragging a broken body across the hardwood floors.

Arthur threw off the covers. He wasn't going to cower in bed from his own subconscious. He grabbed the heavy brass flashlight he kept in the nightstand and stepped out into the dark hallway. 

"Is someone there?" he called out, his voice sounding thin and weak, belying his intended confidence. 

The dragging sound stopped. Total silence descended on the house again. 

He moved toward the staircase landing, his bare feet silent on the carpet. He flicked the switch for the downstairs lights. Nothing happened. The breaker must have tripped. 

He clicked on the flashlight, the harsh beam cutting through the gloom, illuminating the expanse of the living room below. Empty. The furniture remained exactly as he had arranged it.

He began to descend the stairs, the brass flashlight heavy in his grip. He told himself he was just clearing the house. Ensuring the locks held. 

He reached the bottom of the stairs and swept the beam across the kitchen, into the dining room. Nothing. 

He let out a shaky breath, a grim smile touching his lips. His mind playing tricks. That was all. He turned to head back upstairs.

He stopped, the flashlight beam hitting the hallway floor leading toward the basement door. 

There was a streak on the polished wood. A long, dark, smeared trail that glistened wetly in the light. 

Arthur’s stomach bottomed out. The trail led directly from the basement door, across the hall, and vanished into the shadows beneath the stairs he had just descended.

The dragging sound started again. Not downstairs anymore. 

It was coming from the stairs directly above him.

*Thump... scrape...*

*I'm coming back, Arthur.* The whisper wasn't distant. It was right behind his head, accompanied by the distinct smell of old copper and wet basement earth. 

He didn't turn around. He couldn't. 

He just felt the cold, stiff fingers wrap slowly around his ankle.
