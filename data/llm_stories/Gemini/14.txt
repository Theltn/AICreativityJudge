"Seatbelts, everyone!" Ms. Frizzle chirped, slamming the gearshift on the Magic School Bus into a slot that definitely hadn't been there yesterday. It bore a symbol that looked vaguely like an infinity sign crossed with a hazardous materials warning.

"Please let this be a normal field trip," Arnold groaned, gripping his seat until his knuckles turned white. 

"With the Friz? No way!" the class chorused, though Tim sounded slightly less enthusiastic than usual.

"Today, class, we're exploring the very fabric of reality! We're diving into the subatomic realm to witness the birth of a quark!" Ms. Frizzle yelled over the sudden, violent rattling of the engine. The bus didn't shrink this time. It seemed to vibrate in place, the edges of the vehicle blurring into geometric static.

There was a sound like a rubber band snapping, magnified a billion times, and the world outside the windows vanished, replaced by a swirling, chaotic soup of impossible colors. 

"Fascinating!" Ms. Frizzle leaned over the steering wheel, her gecko dress shifting patterns rapidly. "We've bypassed the atomic structure entirely and entered the quantum foam!"

"Ms. Frizzle?" Dorothy Ann piped up, pointing out the window. "According to my research, quantum foam shouldn't have... teeth."

Ms. Frizzle paused, squinting through the windshield. Outside, swimming through the probability waves, were massive, jagged shapes that seemed to exist in too many dimensions at once. They looked like fractal sharks, and they were circling the bus.

"Oh, my," Ms. Frizzle said, her perpetual smile faltering for a fraction of a second. "That's... unexpected."

"Unexpected?" Arnold shrieked. "You mean 'we're going to be eaten by math' unexpected?"

"Now, Arnold, let's not jump to conclusions. It seems my calculations regarding the dimensional tether were slightly off by a factor of... well, several zeroes." Ms. Frizzle began flipping switches rapidly. The dashboard sparked. "We seem to have slipped past the structural limits of our universe and into the... outside."

"The outside of the universe?" Keesha asked, her voice trembling. "Is there even an outside?"

One of the fractal sharks bumped the side of the bus. The metal groaned, and the physical shape of the bus momentarily warped into a rhombus before snapping back.

"Apparently so," Ms. Frizzle muttered, gripping the wheel. "And it appears a localized ecosystem exists here, feeding on the stray energy leaking from reality. Like us."

The engine sputtered and died. The bus drifted helplessly in the chaotic void. 

"So," Carlos said, trying for his usual bravado but failing miserably. "We're stuck outside the universe, in a broken bus, surrounded by dimensional predators. Ms. Frizzle... what do we do?"

For the first time in Walkerville Elementary history, Ms. Frizzle turned to her class, her hands gripping the silent steering wheel, and looked completely, utterly terrified. 

"Class," she whispered, the chaotic glow of the outside illuminating her pale face. "I... I don't know."

The bus groaned as jaws that shouldn't exist clamped onto the rear axle.
