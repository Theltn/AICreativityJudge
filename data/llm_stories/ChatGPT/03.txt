Journal Entry Number 50

The refrigerator is possessed.

I would like the record to show that I did not leap to that conclusion out of superstition. I arrived there scientifically, through repeated observation, note-taking, and one deeply upsetting casserole.

At 7:14 this morning, I entered the kitchen intending to retrieve orange juice. The refrigerator light came on before I touched the handle. That was unusual, but not yet evidence of malice. Then the note printer attached to the door, which I do not own and which definitely did not exist yesterday, produced a slip of paper reading:

FOR THE WEARY SOUL:
Collard greens. Cornbread. Smothered chicken.
You look spiritually anemic.

I admit the menu sounded excellent.

Further note: the handwriting resembled my grandmother's.

Against my better judgment, I opened the fridge. The shelves had reorganized themselves. Ingredients were arranged by course. Butter sat softened in a ceramic dish. A cast-iron skillet, missing since my divorce, rested on the middle rack beside a bowl of chopped onions sweating despite the cold. From somewhere deep behind the vegetable drawer came humming. Church humming. Slow, approving humming.

I should have backed away then.

Instead, I asked, "What exactly are you?"

The fridge answered by dispensing buttermilk.

Three hours later, I had produced the best meal of my adult life. The greens tasted like forgiveness. The cornbread made me call my sister and apologize for Christmas 2019. I cried over the chicken. Not messy crying, just one dignified tear into the gravy. While I ate, the refrigerator clicked contentedly and released little puffs of air that smelled like nutmeg and old hymnals.

Then, just as I was considering a second helping, the note printer whirred again.

NOW THAT YOUR STOMACH IS FULL,
LET US ADDRESS THE HUNGER BENEATH IT.

I laughed because the alternative was concern.

"Very funny," I said.

The kitchen lights dimmed.

Every appliance turned to face me. I am aware that appliances do not have faces; I am also aware of what I saw. The toaster slots widened like a grin. The microwave clock began counting backward in red digits. The blender started without being plugged in and made a sound like a throat clearing before a sermon.

The refrigerator door opened by itself.

Cold white light spilled over the floor. Inside, behind the milk and leftovers and the lovely peach cobbler it had somehow made while I wasn't looking, there was no back wall. There was depth. Hallway depth. Church-basement-at-midnight depth. Something wet and ancient breathed from within.

The printer spat another note.

I HAVE FED YOUR BODY.
STEP INSIDE AND I WILL FEED WHAT COMES AFTER.

At this point I behaved with admirable clarity. I threw the gravy boat into the abyss, vaulted the dog gate, and locked myself in the bathroom with half a cornbread pan and my phone.

Important observations:

1. The entity cannot cross tile thresholds uninvited.
2. It is currently knocking in a rhythm suspiciously similar to "Amazing Grace."
3. It has started offering dessert.

I can smell sweet potato pie under the door.

If anyone finds this journal, tell the landlord the issue is not electrical, despite what he will claim. Also tell my sister the recipe card is on the counter and she can have the cast-iron skillet.

As for me, I must remember the lesson of Entry Number 50:

Never trust an appliance that understands emotional deprivation.
