Journal Entry Number 50

April 10th

The refrigerator spoke again at 3:17 AM.

I know what you're thinking — "again?" Yes. Again. For those just joining this journal (hi, future insurance adjustor), the Samsung SmartCool 9000 I purchased from a man named Doug at a "barely cursed" estate sale has been... evolving.

Entry 12 was when it first suggested recipes. Normal enough. It scanned its contents and recommended a chicken stir-fry. Fine. Helpful, even. I gave it four stars on the app.

Entry 23 was when the recipes got personal. It told me to make my grandmother's jambalaya. I never entered that recipe. My grandmother never wrote it down. She said she'd take it to the grave, and she did — 2019, rest her soul. The fridge knew it anyway. Ingredient for ingredient. Said the secret was a half-teaspoon of brown sugar and "spite."

Entry 31 was when it stopped suggesting and started insisting. I opened the door for a midnight snack, and the screen displayed: MAKE THE CORNBREAD. NOT FROM A BOX. FROM YOUR BLOODLINE. I closed the door. The light inside stayed on. It watched me leave.

Entry 41 was when it started cooking by itself. I woke up to the smell of collard greens. The stovetop was on. Pots I didn't own were simmering. The fridge hummed a hymn — not through its compressor, through something deeper. The greens were perfect. I ate them out of fear.

Which brings me to last night. Entry 50.

3:17 AM. The kitchen light flickers on. I hear the fridge door open by itself. I walk in because I am apparently incapable of learning from forty-nine prior journal entries.

The screen displays a recipe. No title. Just ingredients:

- One cup heavy memory
- Three tablespoons of Sunday morning
- A pinch of the first song you ever heard
- Your mother's laughter, rendered
- Soul (yours), to taste

I stared at it. The fridge stared back — and I know it was staring because the camera light was on and the screen displayed the words I SEE YOU, BABY.

Then I felt it. A pulling. Like someone had hooked a thread behind my sternum and was reeling it inward, toward the vegetable crisper. My chest went cold. My vision narrowed. The fridge began to hum again — that hymn, low and sweet, the one my grandmother used to sing while she cooked, the one I'd forgotten until this cursed appliance remembered it for me.

I grabbed the counter. I said, "No."

The fridge said, "Just a taste."

I unplugged it. The humming continued for four more seconds, then stopped. The screen went dark except for one final message:

I'LL KEEP IT WARM FOR YOU.

I am writing this from the bathroom. The bathroom does not have appliances with opinions. The bathroom is safe.

New house rules:
1. Do not enter the kitchen after dark.
2. Do not make eye contact with the refrigerator.
3. Do not eat anything the refrigerator cooks, no matter how good it smells.
4. Call Doug. Ask about the return policy. Ask about the previous owner. Ask why the estate was "barely" cursed.
5. Buy a cooler. A regular, stupid, non-sentient cooler.

Must remember to avoid kitchen.

The fridge just turned itself back on. I can hear the hymn through the wall.

It's making cornbread.
