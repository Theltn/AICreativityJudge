The first time I saw the shadow, it wasn’t doing anything unusual.

It was just standing there.

Which would’ve been fine—shadows stand still all the time—except this one wasn’t mine.

I was brushing my teeth, half-asleep, when I noticed it on the bathroom wall. Tall. Thin. Shoulders too sharp. Head tilted at an angle no human could manage.

I turned around.

Nothing.

When I turned back, the shadow was gone.

I blamed exhaustion.

The second time, it waved.

I was in the kitchen, pouring cereal, when I saw it cast across the floor. My own shadow was in front of me. This one was behind me.

It lifted its hand.

Slowly.

Deliberately.

I dropped the cereal.

The shadow froze, then melted into the darkness beneath the table.

I didn’t sleep that night.

The third time, it spoke.

I was lying in bed, staring at the ceiling, when a voice whispered from the corner of the room.

"You’re awake."

I sat up so fast I got dizzy. "Who’s there?"

The shadow peeled itself off the wall like wet paint.

It stretched, limbs unfolding like a spider’s.

"Finally," it said. "You can see me."

I scrambled backward. "What do you want?"

It tilted its head. "To help."

"Help with what?"

"Everything you’re afraid of."

My mouth went dry. "I’m not afraid."

It laughed softly. "You’re always afraid. Humans are made of fear."

It stepped closer. The room grew colder.

"I can take it from you," it whispered. "All of it. Every fear. Every worry. Every nightmare."

"And what do you get?" I asked.

Its smile stretched impossibly wide.

"A place to live."

I shook my head. "No."

The shadow paused. "You refuse?"

"Yes."

It sighed, disappointed. "Most people say yes."

"I’m not most people."

It considered this.

Then it stepped backward, melting into the wall.

"Very well," it said. "But I’ll be here."

"Why?"

"Because you cast me. And you always will."

The room went silent.

I didn’t see it again after that.

But sometimes, when the lights are off and the room is quiet, I feel something standing just behind me.

Not threatening.

Not angry.

Just waiting.
