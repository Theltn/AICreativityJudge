The guards strapped me into the electric chair with the kind of efficiency that only comes from routine. Leather cuffs tightened around my wrists and ankles. The metal cap settled onto my shaved scalp, cold as a grave. I could hear my heartbeat in my teeth.

I didn’t commit the murder. Everyone in this room knew it. But the machine of justice had momentum, and I was just another cog crushed beneath it.

The executioner stepped forward, face hidden behind a black hood. “Any last words?”

“Yeah,” I said. “You’re killing the wrong man.”

He didn’t respond. They never do.

He flipped the switch.

And the world went dark.

Not metaphorically. Literally.

The lights died. The hum of the generator choked out. The fans stopped spinning. The room plunged into a silence so complete it felt like a vacuum.

Someone cursed. Someone else stumbled. A guard’s flashlight flicked on, jittery and weak.

“What the hell happened?” the warden barked.

I sat there, still strapped in, very much alive.

Then the emergency lights flickered on—dim, red, pulsing like a heartbeat.

And that’s when we heard it.

A scraping sound. Slow. Deliberate. Coming from somewhere deep in the walls.

The guards froze.

The scraping grew louder, closer, like something dragging metal claws along concrete.

“Is that… part of the generator?” one guard whispered.

“No,” the executioner said quietly. “The generator’s outside.”

The scraping stopped.

Then a voice—distorted, layered, like several people speaking at once—echoed through the room.

**“Innocent.”**

The temperature dropped. Frost crept across the floor toward the chair.

Toward me.

The warden backed away. “What is this? Some kind of trick?”

The voice spoke again, louder.

**“INNOCENT.”**

The leather straps snapped open on their own. The metal cap lifted off my head and clattered to the floor.

I stood, legs shaking.

The red lights flickered wildly, then burst into blinding white.

And for a moment—just a moment—I saw a shape in the glare. Tall. Humanoid. But wrong. Too many joints. Too many eyes. A thing that should not fit inside the geometry of a room.

It pointed at me.

Then at the others.

The lights died again.

Screams filled the darkness.

When the power finally returned, I was standing alone in the execution chamber. The guards were gone. The warden was gone. The executioner’s hood lay on the floor, empty.

The door swung open by itself.

A whisper brushed my ear.

**“Run.”**

So I did.
