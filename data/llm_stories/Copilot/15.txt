The storm hit at midnight.

Not a normal storm—no thunder, no lightning, no rain. Just wind. A howling, furious wind that rattled the windows and made the walls tremble like they were afraid.

I couldn’t sleep. No one could’ve.

At 12:17 a.m., the power went out.

At 12:19, something knocked on my door.

Three slow knocks.

I froze in bed. No one should’ve been outside in that wind. No one could’ve stood upright in it.

The knocking came again.

I grabbed my flashlight and crept toward the door. The beam flickered as if it didn’t want to see what was waiting.

"Who’s there?" I called.

No answer.

Just the wind screaming through the trees.

I opened the door.

A man stood on my porch.

Tall. Thin. Drenched in shadows even though there was no light. His coat whipped violently in the wind, but he didn’t move. Not even a sway.

"May I come in?" he asked.

His voice was calm. Too calm.

"It’s dangerous out here."

I swallowed. "Who are you?"

He tilted his head. "A traveler."

"From where?"

He smiled faintly. "Farther than you can imagine."

The wind roared behind him, bending the trees nearly in half. But he stood perfectly still.

"Please," he said. "I need shelter."

Every instinct screamed at me to shut the door.

But something else—curiosity, maybe—kept me rooted.

"Alright," I said. "Come in."

The moment he stepped inside, the wind stopped.

Not slowed.

Stopped.

The house fell silent.

He looked around, studying everything with unsettling interest.

"You live alone," he said.

It wasn’t a question.

"Yes."

He nodded. "Good."

I stiffened. "Why is that good?"

He didn’t answer. Instead, he walked to the window and peered out at the suddenly still night.

"They won’t follow me in here," he said.

"Who?"

He turned toward me.

His eyes were wrong.

Not glowing. Not monstrous.

Just… too old.

Older than the storm. Older than the world.

"The wind," he said softly. "It hunts."

I stared at him. "Wind doesn’t hunt."

"This one does."

He stepped closer.

"And it wants me."

A chill crawled up my spine. "Why?"

He smiled again—sad this time.

"Because I stole something from it."

"What did you steal?"

He leaned in.

"My freedom."

Before I could respond, the wind slammed against the house with a force that shook the floorboards.

The traveler’s expression hardened.

"It found me."

The windows rattled. The walls groaned. The front door bulged inward as if something enormous pressed against it.

"What do we do?" I whispered.

He placed a hand on my shoulder.

"You run."

"What about you?"

He looked toward the door.

"I stop it."

The wind screamed.

The lights flickered back on.

And the traveler stepped toward the door.

"Thank you for the shelter," he said.

Then he opened it.

The wind swallowed him whole.

And just like that—

The storm ended.
