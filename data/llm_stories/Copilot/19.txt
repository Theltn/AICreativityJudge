The forest wasn’t on any map.

I checked three times.

GPS showed a blank patch of green. No trails. No roads. No name.

But the sign nailed to the crooked wooden post at the entrance said:

**WELCOME TO WISHWOOD**

Which was ridiculous, because forests don’t just appear overnight.

And yet… this one had.

I stepped past the sign. The air changed immediately—cooler, sweeter, humming with a faint vibration I felt in my teeth.

The trees were enormous, their trunks twisting like braided rope. Their leaves shimmered with colors I didn’t have names for.

A narrow path wound deeper into the woods.

I followed it.

After a few minutes, I heard whispering.

Not wind.

Not animals.

Words.

**“What do you want?”**

I froze. "Who said that?"

The trees rustled.

**“What do you want?”** the voice repeated.

It wasn’t a single voice. It was hundreds. Thousands. Layered together like a chorus.

"I don’t… I don’t know," I said.

The forest laughed.

A soft, rustling sound.

**“Everyone wants something.”**

I swallowed. "What are you?"

**“Wishwood.”**

"That’s not an answer."

**“It is the only answer.”**

The path split ahead—one trail glowing faintly gold, the other pulsing with a deep, unsettling red.

**“Choose,”** the forest whispered.

"Choose what?"

**“Your want.”**

I stepped toward the golden path.

It brightened.

Warmth washed over me—memories of childhood summers, my mother’s laugh, the feeling of being safe.

**“This way leads to what you lost,”** the forest said.

My throat tightened.

I turned toward the red path.

It throbbed like a heartbeat.

Cold air spilled from it.

**“This way leads to what you fear.”**

I stepped back. "Why would anyone choose that?"

The forest’s voice softened.

**“Because some fears must be faced to be undone.”**

I stood between the two paths, heart pounding.

"What happens if I choose neither?"

The forest went silent.

Then:

**“Then you stay.”**

The trees shifted, closing in.

Roots curled across the ground like fingers.

**“Wishwood keeps the undecided.”**

Panic surged through me.

I looked at the golden path.

At the red path.

At the forest creeping closer.

And I made my choice.
