The fog rolled in thick over San Francisco Bay, swallowing the Golden Gate Bridge until only its tips pierced the gray. Tourists complained. Locals shrugged. Fog was normal.

What wasn’t normal was the man rising out of the water.

It started as a ripple. Then a swell. Then a towering column of churning ocean that broke apart to reveal a figure—tall, bearded, wild-eyed, and very much alive.

Rasputin.

He stepped onto the pier barefoot, dripping seawater and centuries of myth. His beard hung in tangled ropes. His eyes glowed with a feverish intensity that made onlookers stumble back.

A fisherman dropped his tackle box. "Holy— is that—?"

Rasputin inhaled deeply, savoring the air like a man tasting wine. "A century," he murmured in Russian. "I sleep for one century, and the world becomes… this."

Phones came out. Cameras clicked. Someone livestreamed.

Rasputin squinted at a scooter zipping by. "Strange magic."

A police officer approached cautiously. "Sir, I’m going to need you to—"

Rasputin raised a hand.

The officer froze mid-step, eyes wide, body rigid.

Gasps rippled through the crowd.

Rasputin smiled. "Your laws do not bind me."

He walked past the immobilized officer, leaving wet footprints that steamed on the concrete. People parted instinctively, as if pushed by an invisible force.

A woman whispered, "Is he dangerous?"

A man replied, "He’s Rasputin. Of course he’s dangerous."

Rasputin paused at a street vendor selling hot dogs. He sniffed the air, intrigued. "Meat… in bread?"

"Uh… yeah," the vendor stammered.

Rasputin took one without paying. The vendor wisely did not protest.

He bit into it and nodded approvingly. "The future has its merits."

Then he turned toward the city skyline—glass towers, neon signs, drones buzzing overhead.

"I feel the pulse of this place," he said. "A great power sleeps beneath it. A power I can use."

The crowd exchanged uneasy glances.

A reporter pushed forward. "Grigori Rasputin! What are your intentions here?"

He looked at her with unsettling calm. "To finish what I began."

"Which is?"

He smiled.

"To reshape the world."

The fog thickened around him, swirling like a living thing. When it cleared, he was gone.

But the city felt different—charged, humming, as if something ancient had awakened beneath its streets.

And somewhere in the distance, faint but unmistakable, came the sound of laughter.
