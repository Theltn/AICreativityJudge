The man at the bus stop shouldn’t have been there.

Not because it was late.

Not because the buses had stopped running hours ago.

But because he wasn’t casting a shadow.

I noticed it immediately. The streetlight above us buzzed, spilling pale yellow light across the sidewalk. My shadow stretched behind me.

His did not.

He stood perfectly still, hands folded in front of him, wearing a dark coat that looked older than the city itself.

"Long night," he said without turning.

I swallowed. "Yeah."

"You’re waiting for something." His voice was calm, almost kind.

"A ride," I said. "But I think I missed the last bus."

He finally looked at me.

His eyes were wrong.

Not glowing. Not monstrous.

Just… empty. Like windows into a room with no walls.

"You didn’t miss it," he said. "It’s just late."

I checked the schedule posted on the pole.

"It’s three hours late."

"Some things arrive when they’re meant to, not when they’re scheduled."

A cold breeze swept through the street. The man didn’t react.

"Do you live around here?" I asked.

"I don’t live anywhere."

"Okay… then what are you doing at a bus stop?"

He smiled faintly.

"Waiting for you."

My stomach dropped. "Why?"

He stepped closer.

Still no shadow.

"Because your time is changing."

"My time?"

"Everyone reaches a moment when the path splits. Tonight is yours."

I took a step back. "Look, I don’t know who you are—"

"I’m a guide," he said. "Of sorts."

"A guide to what?"

He pointed down the street.

A bus was approaching.

But it made no sound.

Its headlights were dim, like dying stars. Its windows were black. Too black.

"That’s not a city bus," I whispered.

"No," he agreed. "It’s not."

The bus slowed to a stop in front of us. The doors hissed open.

The interior was dark.

Too dark.

"You have a choice," the man said. "Get on, or walk away."

"What happens if I get on?"

"You go where you’re meant to go."

"And if I walk away?"

He tilted his head.

"Then you stay where you’ve always been."

The bus waited.

Silent.

Patient.

Hungry.

I looked at the man.

At the bus.

At the empty street around us.

And I made my choice.
